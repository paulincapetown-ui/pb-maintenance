VIEW & MANAGE ALL JOBS

2

Signing In (First Visit & Daily Use)

Both pages are protected by a sign-in screen. You sign in with your own @propertybureau.co.za email address.

FIRST VISIT — CREATE YOUR PASSWORD

  1. 1
    Open either link above. The Sign In screen appears.
  2. 2
    Type your work email address exactly (e.g. yourname@propertybureau.co.za) and click Continue.
  3. 3
    You will see "Welcome — first time logging in". Choose a password of at least 8 characters, enter it twice, and click Set Password & Sign In.
  4. 4
    You are now signed in. Your session lasts 8 hours, after which you simply sign in again with your email and password.
If you see "Email not recognised": check your typing — the address must be your exact work email, all dots in place (a common slip is a comma instead of a dot, e.g. co,za). If it still fails, contact Paul to confirm your account.
Keep your password private. Do not share logins — each staff member has their own account, and actions in the system are tied to it.
3

Logging a New Maintenance Job

Use this whenever a tenant or landlord reports a maintenance issue. Takes about 60 seconds. Only three fields are required — Date Logged, Property Name, and Issue Description — fill in the rest as you have it.

  1. 1
    Open the Log New Job page (sign in if asked).
  2. 2
    Check the Date Logged (it defaults to today) and leave Status on Logged for a new job.
  3. 3
    Enter the Property Name — e.g. 12 Beach Road, Sea Point.
  4. 4
    Describe the problem clearly in Issue Description, and add what needs doing in Task / Correction Required if known.
  5. 5
    Enter contractor details if already known (leave blank if not yet assigned).
  6. 6
    Add landlord and tenant names and contact details — use + Add more contacts for extra emails or phone numbers.
  7. 7
    Click Log Job. A confirmation screen shows the Job ID (e.g. PB-20260604-1234) — the job is saved.
Tip: Contractor details can always be added later from the dashboard — don't hold up logging the job waiting for them.
4

Using the Dashboard

The dashboard shows every job. Use it to check status, update progress, and communicate with contractors, landlords, and tenants. It refreshes automatically every 30 seconds.

  1. 1
    Open the Dashboard — it loads the latest data automatically.
  2. 2
    Use the search bar to find a job by property, tenant, or contractor name.
  3. 3
    Use the Status filter to narrow to jobs at a specific stage.
  4. 4
    Click any job to expand its full details and reveal all actions.
  5. 5
    Change the Status dropdown and click Save to update progress.

JOB STATUS STAGES

Logged In Progress Awaiting Parts Complete

Completed jobs stay in the system permanently — use the status filter to show or hide them. Move jobs forward as work progresses — this keeps everyone's view accurate.

5

Sending Emails and WhatsApp Messages

From any expanded job on the dashboard you can contact the relevant party with one click. The system pre-fills the message with job details — you just review and send.

✉️

Email

Click the Email button on the job. Your email client opens with the message and job details already filled in. Review, edit if needed, and send.

💬

WhatsApp

Click the WhatsApp button. WhatsApp Web opens in a new tab with the message pre-filled. Click Send.

AVAILABLE FOR EACH JOB

Contractor Landlord Tenant
Standard sequence: Email the contractor first to confirm they've accepted the job → then notify the tenant with the expected visit date → update the landlord once work is complete.
6

Viewing Email History

Every email sent through the dashboard is logged against the job automatically.

  1. 1
    Expand the job on the dashboard.
  2. 2
    Scroll down to the Email History section to see all messages sent, with timestamps and recipients.
Tip: Before sending a follow-up, check the history to see what was already communicated and when.
7

Deleting a Job

  1. 1
    Find the job on the dashboard and expand it.
  2. 2
    Click the red Delete button at the bottom of the record.
  3. 3
    Confirm when prompted.
Warning: Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Only delete test entries or jobs logged in error. For finished jobs, use Complete status instead.
8

Best Practices

Keep Status Current

Update the job status every time something changes. This keeps Paul and the rest of the team informed without extra calls or messages.

Detailed Descriptions

The more detail in the problem description, the easier it is for contractors to quote accurately and arrive prepared.

Urgent Issues = Same Day

Any safety or urgent issue (no power, no water, leaks, security) must be actioned the same day — assign a contractor and notify the tenant immediately.

Mark Jobs Complete

Once a job is done and invoiced, set its status to Complete. This keeps the active list accurate and uncluttered.
