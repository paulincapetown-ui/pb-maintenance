2

Logging a New Maintenance Job

Use this whenever a tenant or landlord reports a maintenance issue. Takes about 60 seconds.

  1. 1
    Open the Log New Job page.
  2. 2
    Enter the property address and select the landlord.
  3. 3
    Enter the tenant name and tenant contact number.
  4. 4
    Describe the issue clearly in the Problem Description field.
  5. 5
    Select a Priority — use High for any safety or urgent issue.
  6. 6
    Enter contractor details if already known (leave blank if not yet assigned).
  7. 7
    Click Submit Job. A confirmation message confirms it has saved.
Tip: Contractor details can always be added later from the dashboard — don't hold up logging the job waiting for them.
3

Using the Dashboard

The dashboard shows every active job. Use it to check status, update progress, and communicate with contractors, landlords, and tenants.

  1. 1
    Open the Dashboard — it loads the latest data automatically.
  2. 2
    Use the search bar to find a job by property, tenant, or contractor name.
  3. 3
    Use the Status filter to narrow to jobs at a specific stage.
  4. 4
    Click any job to expand its full details and reveal all actions.
  5. 5
    Change the Status dropdown and click Save to update progress.

JOB STATUS STAGES

New In Progress Awaiting Parts Completed Closed

"Closed" means invoiced and fully resolved. Move jobs forward as work progresses — this keeps everyone's view accurate.

4

Sending Emails and WhatsApp Messages

From any expanded job on the dashboard you can contact the relevant party with one click. The system pre-fills the message with job details — you just review and send.

✉️

Email

Click the Email button on the job. Your email client opens with the message and job details already filled in. Review, edit if needed, and send.

💬

WhatsApp

Click the WhatsApp button. WhatsApp Web opens in a new tab with the message pre-filled. Click Send.

AVAILABLE FOR EACH JOB

Contractor Landlord Tenant
Standard sequence: Email the contractor first to confirm they've accepted the job → then notify the tenant with the expected visit date → update the landlord once work is complete.
5

Viewing Email History

Every email sent through the dashboard is logged against the job automatically.

  1. 1
    Expand the job on the dashboard.
  2. 2
    Scroll down to the Email History section to see all messages sent, with timestamps and recipients.
Tip: Before sending a follow-up, check the history to see what was already communicated and when.
6

Deleting a Job

  1. 1
    Find the job on the dashboard and expand it.
  2. 2
    Click the red Delete button at the bottom of the record.
  3. 3
    Confirm when prompted.
Warning: Deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Only delete test entries or jobs logged in error. For finished jobs, use Closed status instead.
7

Best Practices

Keep Status Current

Update the job status every time something changes. This keeps Paul and the rest of the team informed without extra calls or messages.

Detailed Descriptions

The more detail in the problem description, the easier it is for contractors to quote accurately and arrive prepared.

High Priority = Same Day

Any job logged as High priority must be actioned the same day — assign a contractor and notify the tenant immediately.

Close Completed Jobs

Once a job is invoiced and resolved, mark it Closed. This keeps the active list accurate and uncluttered.
